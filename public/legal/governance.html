Governance Model and Pilot Controls

Effective date: March 17, 2026

Contact: cityreport.io@gmail.com

This page summarizes CityReport.io governance commitments for public pilot visibility. It is informational and does not replace executed municipal contracts.

1. Platform Role

CityReport.io operates as an intermediary reporting and accountability platform. We intake reports, support lifecycle tracking, and route information to responsible entities. CityReport.io does not directly perform repairs and is not an emergency response provider.

Non-emergency use only. Reports submitted through CityReport.io are not a substitute for 911 or local emergency services.

2. Pilot Scope

Current municipal pilot reporting scope centers on potholes and water-drain issues. Streetlight and street-sign workflows may exist in platform tooling, but activation depends on municipal configuration and partner approvals.

3. Data Responsibility Model

  • For municipal pilot data, municipalities generally act as controllers for public-service handling decisions.
  • CityReport.io generally acts as a processor or intermediary operator under platform and contract terms.
  • Role allocation can vary by agreement and specific workflow context.

4. Forwarding and Delivery Boundaries

  • Report data may be forwarded to city departments, utilities, or other responsible entities.
  • Forwarding uses operational channels that can include email delivery workflows.
  • Forwarding does not create a guarantee of agency receipt, response time, or resolution outcome.

5. Public vs Internal Visibility

  • Public-facing outputs are designed to provide summary accountability signals.
  • Internal operational views may contain additional detail needed for municipal handling and moderation.
  • Access controls are used to limit exposure of sensitive operational context.

6. Data Handling and Subprocessors

Core subprocessors and infrastructure include:

  • Supabase (storage, auth, backend data services)
  • Google Maps APIs (geospatial and map functions)
  • Resend (email transmission)

Data categories can include user contact details, location data, report content/history, uploaded media, lead capture records, and anti-abuse metadata.

7. Retention and Review

Retention follows policy windows by data class and may vary by agreement, legal requirement, and pilot needs. See Privacy Notice for current public retention targets and rights process.

8. Risk and Liability Positioning

  • No guaranteed response times, repair completion, or outcome accuracy.
  • Platform moderation and anti-abuse controls are applied to reduce false-reporting and misuse risk.
  • Contractual liability allocation and indemnity terms are defined in municipal agreements, not this summary.
